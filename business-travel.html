Best Hotels for Business Travel in Austin

If you're in Austin for work, where you stay depends on which corridor you're working in. This is the practical breakdown.

Austin sprawls. A hotel that's 5 minutes from one office park is 45 minutes from another. The four districts below cover where most business travel demand sits — pick the one closest to your meetings.

Downtown CBD

State government, plus law, consulting, and finance offices clustered around Congress Avenue — and the Austin Convention Center. Walkable, on the lake, premium during big events and conferences.

Anchors: Texas State Capitol, Austin Convention Center, Frost Bank Tower

See Downtown CBD hotels

The Domain / North Austin

Austin's tech corridor: Apple's large campus, Google, Indeed, Amazon, plus Q2 Stadium. About 20 minutes from downtown, and hotels here typically run cheaper than downtown for comparable quality.

Anchors: Apple Austin campus, The Domain, Q2 Stadium

See The Domain / North Austin hotels

Southeast / ABIA

Hotels along State Highway 71 near Austin-Bergstrom serve early flights — and put you close to Tesla's Gigafactory and the Circuit of the Americas (Formula 1, MotoGP, big concerts) southeast of town.

Anchors: Austin-Bergstrom International (AUS), Tesla Gigafactory Texas, Circuit of the Americas

See Southeast / ABIA hotels

Round Rock / North I-35

The suburban corridor north of the city — Dell's headquarters, the Kalahari indoor waterpark resort, and the Round Rock Express ballpark. Roughly 25 minutes from downtown and meaningfully cheaper.

Anchors: Dell Technologies HQ, Kalahari Resorts Round Rock, Dell Diamond

See Round Rock / North I-35 hotels

Booking tips for business travelers

  • Book direct with brand programs if you're loyalty-eligible — Hilton Honors, Marriott Bonvoy, IHG One Rewards. The point earnings + status credit usually beats any 3rd-party discount.
  • Cancellation flexibility matters more than rate — meeting schedules shift. Filter for free cancellation when comparing.
  • Watch for resort fees and parking charges. Some hotels in business districts add $25–45/night in mandatory fees that aren't in the headline rate.
  • Wi-Fi quality varies wildly — premium-tier rooms often include faster Wi-Fi as a built-in amenity. Worth $10–20/night if you're on calls.
