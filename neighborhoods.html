Where to Stay in Austin

Austin isn't one experience — it's six or seven. Pick the neighborhood that matches your trip, then compare hotel prices in that area.

This guide covers the main hotel-dense neighborhoods in Austin, TX, what each is known for, and what kind of trip each one is best for. Click any "See hotels" button to compare live prices on Booking.com filtered to that area.

Downtown / 2nd Street District

High-rise hotels, the 6th Street entertainment district, the Texas Capitol, the Austin Convention Center, and the Lady Bird Lake hike-and-bike trail all within a walk. The 2nd Street District is the polished retail-and-restaurant slice on the west side. Loud near 6th, calmer toward the lake.

Best for: First-time visitors, convention attendees, nightlife

See hotels in Downtown / 2nd Street District

South Congress (SoCo)

The postcard Austin strip — vintage shops, food trailers, the "I love you so much" wall, the neon Austin Motel sign, and a cluster of design-forward boutique hotels. A short walk or ride across the river from downtown.

Best for: Design-conscious travelers, shoppers, couples

See hotels in South Congress (SoCo)

Rainey Street

A block of converted bungalow bars wrapped by new high-rise hotels at the southeast edge of downtown — steps from the convention center and the lakefront trail. Walkable nightlife with a hotel pillow nearby.

Best for: Nightlife, conventions, walkable downtown stays

See hotels in Rainey Street

East Austin

Just east of I-35: taquerias, breweries, galleries, and music venues, plus a fast-growing roster of boutique stays. More local and less polished than downtown, and walkable in pockets.

Best for: Locals' Austin, music and food lovers, longer stays

See hotels in East Austin

Zilker / South Lamar

Near Zilker Park, Barton Springs Pool, and the original Alamo Drafthouse — and the staging ground for ACL Fest. Leafy and residential, with a quick ride into downtown.

Best for: ACL Festival, families, outdoorsy trips

See hotels in Zilker / South Lamar

The Domain (North Austin)

An open-air shopping-and-office district near the Apple, Google, and Indeed campuses and Q2 Stadium — "Austin's second downtown." About 20 minutes from the city center, with hotels that run cheaper than comparable downtown rooms.

Best for: Business travelers in the tech corridor, shoppers, soccer fans
